Computational Biology & Network Science

Understanding complex biological systems through data-driven approaches

I'm a Postdoctoral Research Associate at the University of Connecticut, Department of Ecology and Evolutionary Biology. With 5 years of postdoctoral experience, I apply network science, statistical machine learning, and mathematical modeling to understand evolutionary adaptation, gene regulation, and biological interactions. My work spans from tracking exotic species dispersal networks to characterizing gene co-expression dynamics in natural populations.

Current Research

Gene Co-expression Networks in Wild Populations

Threespine Stickleback Temporal Dynamics

Analyzing comprehensive temporal gene expression data from 2,500+ wild threespine stickleback samples across Alaska lake populations (2019-2023). Characterizing how gene co-expression networks reorganize through time and across populations, examining both mean expression changes and regulatory network architecture. Using batch effect correction (ComBat) and co-expression network analysis to identify population-specific modules.

RNA-seq Co-expression Networks Evolutionary Adaptation Population Genomics

Network Approaches to Biological Invasions

Tracking Exotic Species Dispersal

Using network analysis to characterize and predict human-mediated dispersal of exotic species. Applying graph embedding and machine learning methods to understand interaction patterns in visitation networks, identifying high-risk pathways for unintended species introduction and informing biosecurity interventions.
