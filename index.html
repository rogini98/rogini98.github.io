Effects of Hybridization and Gene Flow on Gene Co-expression Networks

Rogini Runghen, Daniel I. Bolnick

Genetics, 2025, 230(2), iyaf057

Gene co-expression networks infer functional relationships between genes from transcriptomic data. However, hybridization and gene flow can create spurious correlations between genes that are physically linked on chromosomes, not functionally related. Using simulations, we show that these introgression effects significantly alter network structure depending on migration rates and genomic architecture. Since hybridization is common in natural populations, these effects likely shape gene co-expression networks broadly. Our findings emphasize the importance of considering evolutionary history when analyzing co-expression networks.
