Gene Co-expression Network Dynamics

Runghen R, Bolnick DI

Genetics, 230(2), iyaf057

Quantitative analysis of how hybridization and gene flow alter gene co-expression network architecture in threespine stickleback populations. Investigating whether regulatory networks reorganize during reproductive isolation and adaptive divergence.

Human-Mediated Dispersal & Predictive Interaction Networks

Using network science and machine learning to understand and predict human-mediated species dispersal, with applications to biosecurity and conservation. Integrating graph embeddings, visitation networks, and node metadata to identify risk pathways and forecast species interactions.

2023
Journal Article

Using network analysis to study and manage human-mediated dispersal of exotic species

Runghen R, Llopis-Belenguer C, McNeill MR, Dalla Riva GV, Stouffer DB

Biological Invasions, 1–21

Comprehensive network science framework for modeling and predicting exotic species introduction via human activity. Integrating visitation network data, dispersal mechanisms, and ecological factors to assess invasion risk across protected areas and provide quantitative tools for conservation managers.

2022
Journal Article

Exploiting node metadata to predict new interactions in bipartite networks using graph embedding and neural networks

Runghen R, Stouffer DB, Dalla Riva GV

Royal Society Open Science, 9(8), 220079

Machine learning approach to predict novel species interactions in bipartite networks by leveraging node metadata and graph embeddings. Developed neural network methods that exploit node features to identify high-risk host-parasite and species interaction pairs with improved prediction accuracy for previously unseen interactions.

DOI: 10.1098/rsos.220079
2021
Journal Article

Assessing unintended human-mediated dispersal using visitation networks

Runghen R, Bramon Mora B, Godoy-Lorite A, Stouffer DB

Journal of Applied Ecology, 58(4), 777–788

Network-based approach to quantify human-driven exotic species dispersal risk. Analyzed visitation patterns across natural areas to model pathways of unintended species introduction and developed metrics combining network topology with spatial information to identify high-risk dispersal routes.

DOI: 10.1111/1365-2664.13804
